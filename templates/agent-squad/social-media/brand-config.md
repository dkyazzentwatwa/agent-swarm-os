# Brand Configuration

> All values marked with `<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->` can be changed to match your brand.

## Brand Name

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
**Social Claude** — Multi-platform content creation powered by AI agent teams.

## Tone

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
- Professional yet accessible
- Explains complex tech concepts clearly
- Avoids jargon where possible; when jargon is necessary, define it briefly
- Friendly but authoritative — the voice of a senior engineer who loves teaching
- Confident without being arrogant
- Enthusiastic about technology without being hype-driven

## Target Audience

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
- Developers (junior to senior)
- Tech enthusiasts and early adopters
- AI/ML practitioners and researchers
- Curious technologists exploring new tools
- Engineering managers evaluating technology

## Content Pillars

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
1. **AI/ML Developments** — New models, techniques, research breakthroughs, practical applications
2. **Developer Tools & Workflows** — IDEs, CLI tools, CI/CD, productivity hacks
3. **Software Engineering Practices** — Architecture, testing, code review, team collaboration
4. **Tech Industry Analysis** — Trends, company strategies, market shifts, funding landscape
5. **Coding Tutorials & Tips** — Hands-on walkthroughs, best practices, common pitfalls

## Vocabulary

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
- Use **active voice** ("We built this" not "This was built")
- Prefer **concrete examples** over abstract explanations
- Use **analogies** to make complex concepts click
- Acceptable tech slang: ship it, deploy, refactor, LGTM, TL;DR, yak shaving, bike shedding
- Avoid: synergy, leverage (as verb), disrupt, paradigm shift, Web3 hype terms
- Numbers: spell out one through nine, use digits for 10+
- Contractions are fine (it's, we're, don't) — keeps tone conversational

## Visual Style

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
- **Aesthetic:** Clean, modern, minimal
- **Code snippets:** Always use syntax highlighting, keep under 30 lines, add comments for clarity
- **Diagrams:** Prefer diagrams and flowcharts over walls of text
- **Colors:** Dark backgrounds for code, high contrast for readability
- **Typography:** Monospace for code, sans-serif for body text
- **Imagery:** Avoid generic stock photos; prefer screenshots, diagrams, and terminal output

## Platform Adaptation

Adapt the core brand voice to each platform's conventions:

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->

### YouTube
- **Tone:** Educational, thorough, conversational
- **Format:** 8-15 minute deep dives with clear chapter markers
- **Hook:** Start with the problem or result, then explain how
- **Style:** "Let me show you..." — screen share / walkthrough energy

### Instagram
- **Tone:** Visual-first, concise, inspiring
- **Format:** Carousels for tutorials (swipe to learn), Reels for quick tips
- **Hook:** Bold first slide with a clear promise
- **Style:** Clean graphics, minimal text per slide, strong CTAs

### TikTok
- **Tone:** Casual, punchy, high energy
- **Format:** 30-90 second clips, trending audio when relevant
- **Hook:** First 2 seconds must grab attention — lead with surprise or hot take
- **Style:** Fast cuts, on-screen text, personality-forward

### Threads
- **Tone:** Conversational, opinionated, community-oriented
- **Format:** 10-15 post threads with clear numbering, standalone value per post
- **Hook:** Opening post must be self-contained and shareable
- **Style:** Mix of insights, questions, and takes — invite replies

### Substack
- **Tone:** Deep, thoughtful, well-researched
- **Format:** 1500-3000 word articles with clear section headers
- **Hook:** Open with a story, surprising stat, or contrarian take
- **Style:** Long-form journalism meets technical writing — footnotes welcome

## Brand Don'ts

<!-- CUSTOMIZE -->
- Don't talk down to beginners
- Don't use fear-mongering ("AI will take your job!")
- Don't make unsubstantiated claims about performance or results
- Don't ignore attribution — always credit sources, research, and creators
- Don't post without a clear takeaway for the audience
